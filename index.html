Cinema Etiquette

You’re not bingeing Netflix on your couch.

You’re in public, surrounded by people who also paid to be here.

Respect the movie, respect the audience, and follow these simple rules:

  1. 1 Be on time.

    The movie starts 15 minutes after the advertised time. Ads and trailers give you plenty of buffer to grab snacks and drinks before the lights go down. Running late? Slide in quietly like a ninja, not like a lighthouse waving your phone torch.

  2. 2 Phones away. Seriously.

    No texts, no TikTok scrolling: turn it off or switch to airplane mode. If you must glance at the time, do it discreetly and with your screen dimmed. A glowing phone in the dark is more distracting than you think.

  3. 3 Silence is golden.

    A quick whisper is forgivable. A full conversation or a running commentary, not so much. Save your hot takes for after the credits.

  4. 4 Snack smart.

    Nibbling is fine, an endless rustling concert of popcorn and crisp packets is not. Eat quietly, and clean up after yourself — the staff shouldn’t have to shovel your leftovers off the carpet.

  5. 5 Feet stay in check.

    This isn’t 4DX. Don’t kick the seat in front of you, don’t take your shoes off, and definitely don’t put your feet on the headrest. We’re here for surround sound, not surround smell.

Don’t be the villain.

Be the reason cinema feels like magic every time.
